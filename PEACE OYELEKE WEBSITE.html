What is Atelectasis?

Atelectasis is a medical term for collapse of a lung or portion of a lung. Atelectasis can be, resorptive/obstructive, or contraction. Compressive atelectasis is when fluid, air, blood or a tumor presses on alveoli from the outside. Resorptive atelectasis happens when no new air can move into the alveoli (for instance, there's a blockage).

Causes Of Atelectasis

Surgery is the most common cause of atelectasis. Symptoms

The main symptom of atelectasis is difficulty in breathing which may be accompnied by cough, chest pain, or fever.

Reducing The Risks

To reduce the risk of atelectasis it is important to stay active. Also if you are going for surgery, talk to your doctor about ways to prevent atelectasis.
